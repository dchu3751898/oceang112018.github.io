Areas Of Research:

Cyber security comprises of processes, technologies and controls that are designed to protect networks, systems and data from cyber attacks. Cyber security helps in effectively protects individuals and organisations from the unauthorised exploitation of systems, networks and technologies as well as reducing the risk of cyber attacks. Cyber attacks can cause reputation and financial damage that to organisations which can result in a loss of assets, business as well as potential regulatory fines and litigation.

Introduction to Cybersecurity

Hardware authentication:

It is a well-known fact that usernames and passwords used by a majority of data users are weak. This makes it easy for hackers to gain access to information systems and compromise sensitive data of a government agency or business entity. A new Core vPro processor that belongs to the sixth generation of processors has been developed as a solution in the user authentication process. For user identity validation purposes, the core vPro is able to combine different types of hardware components with enhanced factors simultaneously. Intel has dedicated a portion of the processor for security reasons in order to make a device part of the entire process of authentication. When it comes to the Internet of Things (IoT) where the network of connected devices ensures that any device that seeks to be connected has the rights for connectivity to that particular network, hardware authentication can be especially important.

Cloud Technology:

The cloud has a significant impact on the transformation of systems security technology. The number of business enterprises and government agencies that have embraced cloud technology to store the vast amounts of information that they generate on a daily basis has increased. More approaches to information systems security that will be developed for use in the cloud. In addition to that, techniques for on-premise data storage will be migrated to the cloud as components such as virtualized intrusion detection and prevention systems, virtualised systems security and virtualised firewalls are used from the cloud as opposed to traditional forms.

Deep Learning:

Deep learning are encompassed in certain technologies, such as artificial intelligence and machine learning. Deep learning focuses on anomalous behaviour just like behaviour analytics. AI and machine learning systems are able to make decisions on how to prevent hacks without any human input whenever they are fed with the right data regarding potential systems security threats. Instead of users, the system scrutinises entities that have access to the information system. Different entities that are found in the enterprise at both the macro and the micro levels can now be analysed due to the recent developments in machine learning technology. Business organisations and government agencies are now able to overcome any advanced cyber threats through the usage of artificial intelligence and machine learning.

Development in Cybersecurity

The use of AI can help in bolstering cybersecurity and offering more protections against sophisticated hackers. AI helps by automating complex processes for reacting to breaches and detecting attacks. As AI is deployed for security, the applications are becoming more sophisticated. Data deception technology are able to automatically detect, analyse, and defend against advanced attacks. By combining smart security personnel with adaptive technology that continuously change and become smarter over time, a competitive edge is gained by defenders that have been absent from most cybersecurity technologies.

What can robots do?

Robots are electronic devices that are programmed to do a task or tasks that humans may do, ranging from simple industrial robots that help to package products to adaptive robots that can make complex decisions. Most robots will have a method to input, process and output data usually in the form of movement. They also require a physical body and a power supply to run on. There are some robots that are simple and some that are very complex. Robots are designed to do tasks that people may or not be able to do for themselves. They are used to build cars, dismantle bombs, pack-boxes, answer phones, mow yards, explore deep sea ship wrecks and many other things. It’s important to note that robotics technologies may have only reached some level of maturity in recent years, but they’ve existed in the popular imagination for generations, and in some cases even millennia. Today, our world is fairly close to realizing some of the more idealistic aspects of robotics. Walking robots that can communicate verbally with their human owners are a long way off yet, but there are commercially available robots to help with a variety of home tasks. Artificial intelligence has been programmed into vacuum cleaners, non-humanoid personal assistants and even self-cleaning litter boxes. Robots can be programmed not only to perform tasks that we don’t want to complete but also to perform jobs that humans can’t alone. This latter concept is why hospitals and medical facilities may be the setting for more great development in robotics in the coming years.

Technology and Valkyrie

At the Edinburgh Centre for Robotics, work has begun on developing one of the most advanced humanoids on the planet, Valkyrie, a robot which will be used in a future NASA mission to explore the surface of the red planet, Mars. “Valkyrie is a huge scientific undertaking. The researchers are looking forward to tackling the many technical challenges involved in developing a large-scale humanoid robot, and pushing the state of the art in humanoid robotics”. At the moment the robot is a pretty basic shell which can walk up a set of three small steps and can reach out and grip something and pass it on to someone. It reacts if pushed against it, either swaying or taking a step back. The big challenge will be getting Valkyrie to interact with people; it has to has some pretty adaptable algorithms. The main target is to have something that can be a co-worker for astronauts on space missions, for example. Scientists at the University of Edinburgh working on the 6-foot (1.8-meter) tall robot that weighs just under 280 pounds (125 kilograms), hope Valkyrie will be ready for a mission to Mars in as little as five years. Valkyrie is designed to work more readily alongside people that could accompany the robot in future missions. For example, there would be no need for specialized ramps onboard spacecraft to accommodate robot wheels. Valkyrie can walk just like humans, Valkyrie is currently equipped with a pair of stereoscopic cameras, which the robot will use as eyes to explore the surroundings. It contains cameras on its belly, adding additional visual input, as well as a set of sensors that will be sensitive to pressure and touch, allowing Valkyrie to respond to changes if needed. Valkyrie doesn’t have any ears and cannot speak, but is equipped with a special sensory laser radar that’s housed in its face. The laser radar can be used to measure the distances of objects by firing pulses of light towards surfaces and then calculating the time it takes for the light to reflect back, like an echo.

Likely Impact

Rapid advances in computer technology and robotics have resulted in the increasing use of robots in science, medicine and industry. The impact these robots have had on humanity have been both positive and negative. Given the rapid pace of technology, robots will likely have an even greater impact on humans in the future. The practical use of robots in manufacturing has been ongoing since the latter part of the 20th century, with robots used on assembly lines, manufacturing products such as automobiles, food and drugs. Robots have also served scientific purposes, such as the robotic arm, used by Endeavor, a NASA space shuttle. The use of robots in manufacturing has both positive and negative effects. Robots can be used to perform dangerous tasks that would put a person at risk if he were to undertake them, which is clearly beneficial. On the other hand, robots are increasingly being used in place of human workers. Although this can yield higher profits for the manufacturer, the increased unemployment this creates is likely to have a negative effect on the economy as a whole. According to Marshall Brain, author of "Robot Nation" essays, the use of robots will increase substantially in the coming years. Robots will be used to perform jobs currently being done by humans, such as airline pilots, baggage handlers and retail checkout clerks. Brain foresees a "robot nation" in which systems will no longer require any human supervision or intervention, with robots repairing and manufacturing other robots. Brain anticipates the impact on the U.S. economy could be devastating. A scenario in which robots create robots has long intrigued science fiction writers, in which high-tech robots are typically depicted as malevolent entities intent on destroying or enslaving humanity.
